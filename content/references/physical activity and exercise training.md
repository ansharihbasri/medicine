### adverse events: activity-dependent and disease-dependent
- to minimize activity-dependent AE:
	- safety precautions.
	- if doing individually is concerning, then require supervised program at least initially
- to minimize disease-dependent AE:
	- if recommended to do vigorous/high-intensity, follow [[guidelines on exercise testing and prescription]].
	- most ppl w chronic dx can undergo moderate-intensity, but if needed, check dx-specific exercise testing or refer to supervised program (or both)

# [[exercise testing]]
- the idea seems to be to test, through exercise, whether the person is at risk for cardiovascular events, detect CAD, check for capacities at maximal (or 85% max heart rate?) exertion... -> but the general idea is there is at least **two big purposes** of exercise testing: 1. **diagnostic** (diagnosing especially CAD) (see [[physical activity and exercise training#^d10f5b|below]]), and 2. **benchmarking** for crafting **exercise training prescription** (see [[physical activity and exercise training#^603468|alternative exercise testing]])
