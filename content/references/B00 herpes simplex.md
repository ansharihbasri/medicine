[[SNPPDI 4]]

# key concepts
- can undergo latency -> recurrence -> quo [[ad sanationam]] [[dubia (prognosis)]] [[ad malam]] (PPK 298)

# etiology
- [[herpes simplex virus (HSV)-1]] -> predilects [[lips]] or upper side of the body
- [[hepes simplex virus (HSV)-2]] -> predilects [[genitalia]] or lower side of body

# pathomechanism
- can be recurrent (latency)
- transmission: ([WHO](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/herpes-simplex-virus#:~:text=cause%20recurrent%20symptoms.-,Transmission,during%20delivery%2C%20causing%20neonatal%20herpes.))
	- [[herpes simplex virus (HSV)-1]]: contact w virus at sores, saliva, or skin surfaces in or around mouth; less commonly transmitted to genital via oral-genital contact. **can be** transmitted from oral or skin surfaces **that appear normal**.
	- [[hepes simplex virus (HSV)-2]]: sexual contact w genital or anal surfaces, skin, sores or fluids of infected person. can be transmitted even if skin appear normal; **often when asymptomatic**.
