# impostor syndrome
When I think of medical research (given its pragmatism), I feel rather discouraged or not as stimulated (?) but when I compare it to **maths research**, the latter looks **enticing, thrilling.** Now **it may be that I only see the good parts of math** and have been exposed to the rather mundane parts of medicine, but I also think there is a bit of passion thing as well as impostor syndrome. I like maths (idk about its research) because **I know how it feels to learn maths and get ever deeper**. It's addicting, in a good way. Enriching. And even the **parts that make me feel good when I feel good about medical research often is related to math or logical works.** But I've always thought that maths are reserved for those majoring in maths and geniuses while **people like me, despite the hobbyist enthusiasm, may not have what it takes to get into the field professionally.** But what if we overcome that impostor syndrome, and remind ourselves it's entirely possible that I get into that field, maybe with a few years of preparatory training? Now just imagining that feels exciting to me. Also stuff like **programming**! Now those are fruitful fields.

I suppose what I fear when thinking of getting into maths or any other field that are not normally expected from me given my past background is that **I worry if I don't do well in it, people (maybe my parents) would 'blame' me for it.** On the other hand, if I don't do well (or struggle) in a field that was forced onto me, I could make an excuse and say "I never said I wanted to be here!". So I tend to foreclose on possibilities beyond my conventional scope, even if considering many things I may succeed better in those fields.

Perhaps also what I fear in maths and not in medicine is that while I know medicine is destined for a clear, obvious and therefore safe purpose, maths are unclear and thus feels unsafe... Maybe using medicine as problems and maths as tools is the way to go.

# entrepreneurs
Reading about entrepreneurs, creative innovators, always makes me feel mesmerized, fascinated, thrilled by the possibility of me being one too. But it feels very far away, though if I were to focus just on the thrill, it makes me wonder what creative soul within me is trying to speak but which has been for so long been silenced in the name of mundane duties...

# it doesn't need to be dramatic
But again, changes in career doesn't have to be dramatic. I kept being reminded of the fact that what I needed was a change in rhythm, still within the field is fine but just one sub of if that aligns with my lifestyle. It's helpful to think beyond the boundaries, but don't let that bog me down or paralyze me either.

**Maybe just making that small change (just rhythm but not field) is enough as the gentle evolutionary step. Take it slowly.** We're continuously growing and there'll be newer and newer options down the road anyway.
