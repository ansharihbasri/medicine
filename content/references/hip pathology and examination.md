## hip flexion
- first flex **until flattening of lumbar lordosis** bcs before that point flexion may still due to pelvis tilting and lumbar spine arching. after that, the flex is solely from hip joint. (see Bates 1341/2145)
- flexion deformity e.g. due to hip flexion contracture would lead the patient to compensate by tilting the pelvis anteriorly and increasing lumbar lordosis so the torso is slightly more vertical and relief the tension/allow the thigh to be more flat. (see Bates 1341/2145) ^668f9e

## [[FADIR test]] <- [[femoroacetabular impingement (FAI)]]
Flex, adduct, internally rotate the hip (internal rotation of the femur)
[YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xyJUIhsL4lg) ![[Pasted image 20250501132302.png]]
- now why FAI and labral tear are indicated when FADIR test is positive?
	- -> flexion, adduction and internal rotation brings the anterolateral [[femoral neck]] and anterior superior [[acetabular rim]] closest together: so when there's [[femoroacetabular impingement (FAI)]] or [[hip labral tear]] that contact area would be impinged.
		- but when that area is impinged, which pain nerves exactly is triggered and why? or which areas are innervated and triggered when impinged?
			- -> [[hip (acetabular) labrum]], [[hip joint capsule]] (see: [[hip joint capsule#^0209d9|hip joint capsule innervation]]), possibly [[periosteum]] of [[acetabular rim]]
