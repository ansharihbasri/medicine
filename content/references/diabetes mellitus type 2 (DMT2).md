# pathophysiology
- impaired [[insulin]] secretion
- [[insulin resistance]] -> decreased [[glucose]] utilization -> [[impaired glucose tolerance (IGT)]] (increased [[gula darah sewaktu 2 jam postprandial (GDS2PP)]]) (see [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)#^24e6af|below]])
- excessive hepatic [[glucose]] production -> [[impaired fasting glucose (IFG)]] (see [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)#^24e6af|below]])
- abnormal [[fat metabolism]]
- systemic low-grade inflammation

- but **why exactly beta cells can't sustain compensatory hyperinsulinemia**?
	- so in short: increased [[insulin]] production requires more ATP and metabolic activity by the beta cells themselves; but too much ATP production and metabolic activity means more [[ROS (reactive oxygen species)]] produced by mitochondria, and since [[pancreatic beta cell]] particularly have low levels of [[antioxidant]]s (since beta cells' sensitivity to small changes in nutrient levels require minute levels of ROS—not just ATP levels—to be present rather than easily neutralized by antioxidants (see [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)#^cac9d2|below]])), the too much ROS starts damaging the beta cells, which at some point would lead to apoptosis. Also, ER stress due to synthesis of these very many [[insulin]]s result in more misfolded proteins (akin to how overworked employee makes more mistakes—see [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)#^77d385|below]]) also eventually signaling apoptosis to prevent larger damage. In sum, it's due to **ROS damage (due to mitochondrial overwork and beta cell's naturally very low antioxidant levels) and ER stress (due to ER overwork).**
