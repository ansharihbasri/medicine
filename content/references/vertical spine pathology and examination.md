# overview
- pathology: (further read p. 1326/2145)
	- [[lumbar spine osteoarthritis (OA)]] or [[hip osteoarhtritis (OA)]]
	- [[strain]] or [[sprain]] of [[paravertebral muscles]] in lower back
	- [[lumbosacral nerve root compression]]
	- [[lumbosacral spinal cord compression]]
	- mass lesion
	- [[infections]] in the hip, [[rectum]], pelvis
	- [[facet joint]] pathology (see [[vertical spine pathology and examination#^9fcf28|rotation-extension provocation]])

# palpation (seek pain/tenderness, abnormal prominence)
- **palpate each vertebra.**
	- tenderness <- [[fracture]], [[dislocation]], [[infections]], [[arthritis]]
		- further detail on which area correspond to which pathology see p. 1321/2145
	- specifics on each region:
		- on cervical:
			- ...
		- on lumbar:
			- [[vertebral step-off]]s (unusual prominence) <- [[spondylolisthesis]] (forward slippage of one vertebra, may cause compression)
		- on [[sacroiliac joint]] (around the level of [[PSIS (posterior superior iliac spine)]] (see [[sacroiliac joint#^ad6a34]]))
			- tenderness over [[sacroiliac joint]] <- [[sacroiliitis]], [[ankylosing spondylitis]]
		- on paravertebral muscles
			- tenderness
			- spasm: firm, knotted, may be visible <- [[degenerative and inflammatory muscle disorder]], overuse, prolonged contraction from abnormal posture, [[anxiety]]
- any other areas suggested by the patient's symptoms
	- pain radiation to buttocks, [[perineum]], legs
	- [[herniated intervertebral disc]] -> tenderness of spinous processes, [[intervertebral joint]]s, [[paravertebral muscles]], [[sacrosciatic notch]], [[sciatic nerve]] (read further p. 1322/2145)
	- all [[low back pain (LBP)]] assess for possible [[cauda equina compression]] -> risk of limb paralysis or bladder/bowel dysfx (read further p. 1322/2145)

# ROM (provoke pain by movement)
- any pain or tenderness particularly that which radiates to the legs, proceed to [[neurological examination]] of lower extremities
## cervical spine
- limited ROM <- stiffness from [[arthritis]] ([[osteoarthritis (OA)]], pain from [[trauma]], muscle spasm
- neck, shoulder, or arm pain, numbness, or weakness <- possible [[cevical cord compression]] or [[nerve root compression]]

# special maneuvers
## [[Spurling test]] <- [[cervical nerve root compression]]
- modifications available, but most provocative ([PhysioTutors](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3ZSNdv0o0yk)): neck extension and lateral bending to affected side then axial compression ![[Pasted image 20250502065832.png]]
	- this can elicit distal pain seen in [[cervical radiculopathy]] and proximal pain in [[cervical spondylosis (osteoarthritis (OA))]]
