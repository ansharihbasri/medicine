# etiopathogenesis 
- [[Malassezia furfur]] (yeast) -> normal skin flora, but overgrowth (from yeast to hypha form) -> penetrate [[stratum corneum]] -> [[azelaic acid]] produced by [[Malassezia furfur]] (now too much) inhibit [[tyrosinase]] required for [[melanin]] synthesis -> [[hypopigmentation]].
	- also oxidative damage to [[melanocyte]]s -> patchy skin depigmentation
	- some strains may stimulate [[melanin]] production -> [[hyperpigmentation]]
	- hyphal penetration -> mild inflam; increased epidermal turnover -> scaly, fine powdery scales (*"pityron" = bran/fine, powdery scales*)
- inflam is not so much bcs:
	- remain superficial thus does not trigger inflam (like [[B35 dermatophytosis]])
