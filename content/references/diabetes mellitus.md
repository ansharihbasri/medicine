# basic ideas
- involves reduced [[insulin]] secretion, decreased [[glucose]] utilization, or increased [[glucose]] production.
- also see: [[C-peptide and insuline]]

# types
- [[diabetes mellitus type 1]]: autoimmunity against [[pancreatic beta cell]]s -> complete or near-total [[insulin]] deficiency
- [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)]]: involves varying degree of
	- [[insulin]] resistance
	- impaired [[insulin]] secretion
	- increased hepatic [[glucose]] production
- [[prediabetes]] (in context of [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)]]):
	- [[impaired fasting glucose (IFG)]]
	- [[impaired glucose tolerance (IGT)]]
- [[gestational diabetes mellitus]]: development of [[diabetes mellitus]] in second or third trimester due to increased [[insulin]] demand (given physiological resistance);
	- if develop in first trimester, considered preexisting pre-gestational DM.
	- most return to normal [[postpartum]] but with increased risk (35-60%) developing DM in the following 10-20 years (why?)
		- children born to [[gestational diabetes mellitus]] mother is at risk of [[metabolic syndrome]]
		- thus: women with history of [[gestational diabetes mellitus]] undergo lifelong screening for [[diabetes mellitus]]/[[prediabetes]] development every at least 3 years ^0e0564

- updates on current classification:
	- [[insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (IDDM)]] and [[non-insulin dependent diabetes mellitus (NIDDM)]] no longer used since [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)]] eventually (many) needs insulin anyway.
	- age or treatment modality is not a criterion:
		- [[diabetes mellitus type 1]] most commonly **develop before age 30 (or in fact before 20, says p. 2854),** but autoimmunity against [[pancreatic beta cell]] can develop any age (also see: [[diabetes mellitus type 1#^5ec3ba|type 1 DM pathogenesis]]): ^dd3944
			- **5-10%** who develop [[diabetes mellitus]] **after age 30** is [[diabetes mellitus type 1]]
		- [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)]] onset also even covers to younger adults and children now esp [[obesity]]

## criteria
- three categories of [[glucose tolerance]]:
	- [[normal glucose homeostasis]]:
		- [[gula darah puasa (GDP) or fasting plasma glucose (FPG)]] < 100 mg/dL (Harrison) ^bb134b
		- [[gula darah sewaktu 2 jam postprandial (GDS2PP)]] < 140 mg/dL (Harrison) ^57aeb6
		- [[HbA1c]] < 5.7%
	- [[impaired glucose tolerance (IGT)]]
		- [[gula darah puasa (GDP) or fasting plasma glucose (FPG)]] 100-125 mg/dL or 110-125 mg/dL (WHO) ^0cb96b
		- [[gula darah sewaktu 2 jam postprandial (GDS2PP)]] 140-199 mg/dL ^cd63e3
		- [[HbA1c]] 5.7 - 6.4%
	- [[diabetes mellitus]] ^63f9ac
		- ![[Pasted image 20250510084601.png]]
- check with:
	- [[gula darah puasa (GDP) or fasting plasma glucose (FPG)]]
	- [[tes toleransi glukosa oral (TTGO) or oral glucose challenge]]
	- [[HbA1c]]
## screening
- [[gula darah puasa (GDP) or fasting plasma glucose (FPG)]] or [[HbA1c]] is best for asymptomatic individuals and hence for screening
- ADA recommendation: screen every 3 years for:
	- all individuals > 45 y/o
	- \[any\] earlier age if overweight (≥ 23 in [[body mass index (BMI)|Asia-Pacific]]) and have [[diabetes mellitus type 2 (DMT2)#^201007|one additional risk factor for DMT2]]

	- [[infection]]s
	- [[skin pathology]] -> [[dermatologic manifestations of diabetes mellitus]]
