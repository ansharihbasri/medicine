	- [[reactive hyperemia]]: elevated legs get ischemic; the local hypoxia somehow produce and accumulate [[vasodilator]]s which, once the legs put down dangling/dependent position, trigger excessive vasodilation (hence [[reactive hyperemia]]). in normal limb, no ischemia means no need for [[reactive hyperemia]] in the first place, so no rubor.
	- more time it takes to go rubor means more severe [[peripheral artery disease (PAD)]], since it is harder for the blood to go through.
