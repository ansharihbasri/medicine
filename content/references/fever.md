# basic science
- "Feeling cold, goose bumps, and shivering accompany a rising temperature, whereas feeling hot and sweating accompany a falling temperature. Normally, the body temperature rises during the day and falls during the night. When fever exaggerates this swing, night sweats occur."(Bates 13th p. 347/2145)
	- Q: does that mean feverish person still have the same lower bottom set point thus the greater swing? -> continue discussion at [[fever#^9eb79d]]

- chill, shiver when rising temp
	- why? (Guyton 14th p. 910)
		- chill bcs the blood temp is relatively lower than the newly high set point, also skin is vasoconstricted so feels cold.
		- shiver to increase heat production by muscles to attain that new set point
- hot, sweating when falling temp
	- why? (Guyton 14th p. 910)
		- sweating to vent out now excess heat due to lowered back set point;
		- hot also bcs skin is vasodilated everywhere, blood runs around there intensely, feeling hot.
