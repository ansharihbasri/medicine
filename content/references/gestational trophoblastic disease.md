# diseases

	- ([[invasive (hydatidiform) mole]])

	- [[placental site trophoblastic tumor]]
	- [[epithelioid trophoblastic tumor]]

# pathogenesis
- following pregnancy (Williams Obs p. 241) ^25850c
	- 50% after [[hydatidiform mole]]
	- 25% after [[abortion]] or tubal [[ectopic pregnancy]]
	- 25% after [[preterm delivery]] or [[aterm]] pregnancy

# s/sx
- [[irregular bleeding]] a/w uterine subinvolution (Williams Obs p. 241)
	- -> intermittent or continuous, may be sudden and massive

# compli
- [[intraperitoneal bleeding]]/hemorrhage due to myometrial perforation (Williams Obs p. 241)

# risk factor
Williams Obs p. 240, (GTN):
- older maternal age
- preevacuation [[β-hCG]] level > 100,000
- uterine size large for gestational age
- [[theca-lutein cyst]] > 6cm
- slow decline in [[β-hCG]] levels

# diagnosis
- unusually persistent bleeding following any pregnancy should prompt [[β-hCG]] level measurement (Williams Obs p. 241)
	- also assess:
		- uterine size
		- lower genital tract metastases: bluish vascular masses
			- biopsy of these is unnecessary; may cause significant bleeding.
	- lab exam:
		- ([[β-hCG]] level)
		- [ ] [[CBC]]
		- [ ] liver test and renal fx test to check for metastases
		- [ ] [[transvaginal sonography]]
		- [ ] [[chest CT]]/[[radiography]]
		- [ ] brain and abdominopelvic CT
	- 

## staging, scoring
- [[FIGO stage]]
- [[FIGO score]]
- low-risk GTN = FIGO I-III: <7
- high-risk GTN = FIGO II-III: ≥7 and IV
	- ultra high-risk = score ≥13 or w liver, brain, or extensive metastases

# tx
- [[GTN]] often managed as single composite clinical entity (not differentiated into the different histological types) (Williams Obs p. 235)
- before [[chemotherapy]]:
	- if no extrauterine dx is found, second [[curettage]] or [[hysterectomy]] is done, followed w checking [[β-hCG]] level every 2 weeks until 3 consecutive undetectable range. then routinely checked every 6 mo (Williams Obs p. 241)
	- if [[β-hCG]] level persist or if initial extrauterine dx is found, we do staging and then [[chemotherapy]]
[FIGO Cancer Report 2021; Ngan, et al. 2021](https://obgyn.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ijgo.13877)
- low-risk
	- [[single-agent chemotherapy]]
		- [[methotrexate]]
		- [[actinomycin D]]
	- may require additional agents
	- scores 5-6 a/w more drug resistance
- high-risk
	- [[multiagent chemotherapy]]
		- -> most commonly used: [[EMA-CO]]
	- w or w/o adjuvant [[surgery]] for resistant foci excision / brain metastases [[radiotherapy]]
		- [[surgery]]
			- considered in uncontrolled uterine bleeding
				- avoided w uterine [[artery embolization]]
			- [[laparotomy]] maybe to stop bleeding in liver, GI tract, kidneys, spleen
			- [[neurosurgery]] for bleeding into brain or increased [[intracranial pressure (tekanan tinggi intrakranial TTIK)]]
		- [[radiotherapy]]
			- not so much except in brain metastasis tx but controversial efficacy compared w [[intrathecal]] metotrexate
- [[gentle induction chemotherapy]]
- serosurveillance continued 1 year after [[β-hCG]] level undetectable (Williams Obs p. 242)
	- + [[contraceptive]] to avoid teratogenic fx of [[chemotherapy]] + avoid [[β-hCG]] confusion

# education
Williams Obs p. 242
- subsequent pregnancy?
	- w prior [[hydatidiform mole]]:
		- no impaired fertility
		- pregnancy outcomes usually normal
		- 1% risk for trophoblastic dx in subsequent preg (Williams Obs p. 242)
	- w prior [[GTN]]:
		- delay preg for 1 year (most relapses develop during this time)
			- [[methotrexate]] may persist in human tissues for months
			- but still, if pregnant within this time, likely favorable outcome; it's just that if there's relapse, the diagnosis may be delayed during pregnancy
		- 1.5% [[stillbirth]] rate compared w normal 0.8%
