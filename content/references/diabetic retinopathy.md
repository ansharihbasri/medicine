# treatment
- most effective tx is prevention
- intensive glycemic and BP control -> delay development and slow progression
	- in first 6-12 mo of improved gly control, [[diabetic retinopathy]] may transiently worsen; but afterwards improve in the long term
- prophylactic laser photocoagulation for known retinopathy
	- laser photocoagulation -> proliferative retinopathy or [[macular edema]]
	- anti-VEGF therapy (ocular injection)
- if already advanced, improved gly control provide less benefit
- adequate ophthalmologic care can prevent most blindness
- [[fenofibrate]] reduce progression of retinopathy
- regular comprehensive eye exam is essential for all DM
- most can be successfully treated if detected early
