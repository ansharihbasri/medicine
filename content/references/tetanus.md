
# etiopathogenesis 
- [[tetanospasmin]] goes up to [[anterior horn]] or even brainstem via [[retrograde axonal transport]] and, in [[glycinergic interneuron]]s and [[GABA]] secreting neurons of the brainstem, cleave [[SNARE protein]] more specifically [[synaptobrevin]] hence blocking release if [[glycine]] and [[GABA]], causing [[hyperreflexia]], [[muscle spasm]], [[spastic paralysis]].
