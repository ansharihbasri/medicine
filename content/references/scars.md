
- [[hypotrophic or atrophic scar]]: insufficient [[collagen]]
- [[hypertrophic scar]] (too much scar but still within original boundaries): excess [[collagen type III]] during wound healing <– prolonged inflam or mechanical tension on wound (tension signals more collagen is needed to prevent tear) (see: [[purpose of suture]]) ^1c141d
- [[keloid]] (too much scar and outside original boundaries): excessive [[fibroblast]] prolif and [[collagen]] production <- genetic predisposition, ...
- [[hypopigmentation scar]]: loss or damage of [[melanocyte]]s in scar area during injury or healing
- [[hyperpigmentation scar]]: excess melanin production as part of inflam response
