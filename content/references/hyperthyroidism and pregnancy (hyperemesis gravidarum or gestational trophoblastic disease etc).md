
## excessive hCG mimicking [[hyperthyroidism]]
- "[[hyperthyroidism]] from production of [[thyrotropin]] by molar tissue occurs in up to 10% patients w [[hydatidiform mole]]" (Lange book 8th p. 969)
	- ![[human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)#^74b0d7]]
	- thus [[human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)]] can weakly bind to [[TSH receptor]], though only at excessive levels e.g. in [[gestational trophoblastic disease]] can it significantly increase T3/T4 production thus mimicking [[hyperthyroidism]]
# [[hyperthyroidism]] drug choice concern in pregnancy
- [[hyperthyroidism]] tx options are [[propylthiouracil (PTU)]] or [[methimazole]]: the latter has potential teratogenic effect esp in 1st trimester whereas the former has greater hepatotoxicity; perhaps concern in pregnancy is thus judicial use of these drugs: e.g. maybe [[propylthiouracil (PTU)]] in 1st trim but then [[methimazole]] in second and third trim etc. (https://www.drugs.com/pregnancy/propylthiouracil.html, etc.)
