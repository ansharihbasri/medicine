## differential diagnoses

^81a7ae

![[Pasted image 20250429054339.png]]
- [[syncope]]:
	- prodrome: [[lightheadedness]], vision dimming. whereas [[seizure]] often sudden, no warning, followed by confusion (post-ictal phase)
	- trigger: standing, heat, pain, emotional stress. [[seizure]] (esp [[G40 epilepsy and recurrent seizures]]) may be unprovoked
	- flaccid ([[seizure]] may [[convulsion]] or twitching)
- [[psychogenic nonepileptic seizure]]
	- bizarre movements, eye tightly closed, pelvic thrusting, side to side head movements etc (like tantrum?)
	- normal [[electroencephalography (EEG)]] whereas [[G40 epilepsy and recurrent seizures]] seizure is abnormal
	- trigger: emotional stress, ... whereas [[G40 epilepsy and recurrent seizures]] seizure unprovoked
- [[breath-holding attack]]s (in children)
	- trigger: anger/frustration/crying: when crying -> stops breathing -> hypoxia. [[seizure]] may be unprovoked
- [[involuntary movement (movement disorder)]]:
	- no loss of consciousness.
- [[transient ischemic attack (TIA)]]
	- no rhythmic jerking, no convulsions, may be gradual focal deficits
- [[migraine]]
	- maybe similar in aura but lasts longer; [[seizure]] auras are brief
- [[transient global amnesia (TGA)]]
	- no convulsion, no loss of consciousness.
