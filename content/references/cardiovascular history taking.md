- three key questions (Bates 13th p. 838/2145)
	1. pre(self)-cardial: blood supply to the heart (coronary) adequate?
		- [[angina pectoris (chest pain of CAD)]] syndrome exacerbated by exercise or increased need for oxygen
			- [[coronary heart disease]]
	1. intra-cardial: electrical system working well?
		- [[palpitations]]
		- [[abnormal or irregular or extra heartbeat]]
	2. post-cardial: pumping to the rest of the body adequately?
		- LV not working well:
			- [[pulmonary edema]]
			- [[dyspnea with exertion]] or lying flat ([[orthopnea]]) -] inquire [[how many pillows are used at night or during sleep]]
				- [[paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (PND)]] -> inquire [[does it wake you up at night]] or what do you do to make it better (perhaps gasping around, opening the window for air etc)
				- also determine [[NYHA classification of inotropic deficit in chronic heart failure]]: occur in climbing stairs? how many flights? steps? walking? putting on clothes?
			- [[lightheadedness]], [[loss of consciousness]] (brain not enough supply), [[syncope]]
		- RV not working well:
			- [[peripheral edema]]
	- additional consideration:
		- [[valvular heart disease]]: often asymptomatic or related to [[(congestive) heart failure (CHF)]]
