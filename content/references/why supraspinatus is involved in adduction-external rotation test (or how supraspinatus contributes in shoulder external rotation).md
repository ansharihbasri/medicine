

during shoulder external rotation, [[humeral head]] tends to slide superiorly or even anteriorly. to keep this from happening, [[supraspinatus]] pulls the [[humeral head]] downward and compresses it into [[glenoid]], so it is stabilized. hence [[supraspinatus]] integrity is needed for external rotation.

if [[supraspinatus]] is torn, external rotation may trigger misalignment of [[humeral head]], leading to either weakness or pain.
