# biochem
- c/o two dissimilar subunits: alpha and beta (Williams Obs p. 96)
	- [[LH]], [[FSH]], [[TSH]], [[human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)]] all share a common alpha subunit; but each beta subunit is distinct although related amino acid sequence. ^74b0d7
		- -> perhaps why we use beta hCG not alpha bcs that's the distinctive part ^3811a9
			- beta subunit antibody used in most preg test reacts w both: (p. 97)
				- major form in plasma: intact hCG
				- major form in urine: terminal product of hCG degradation i.e. beta core fragment. this degraded product follow the dynamics of intact hCG thus why it can be used as well.
- produced both in [[syncytiotrophoblast]] and [[cytotrophoblast]] before 5 weeks; later in [[first trimester]] however it's produced almost solely in [[syncytiotrophoblast]]. peaks around 9 w then declines to plateau (starting at around 16 w) for the rest. (p. 97)
	- peak maternal plasma level reach approx 50,000 - 100,000 b/w 60-80th days after mens ^3df298
