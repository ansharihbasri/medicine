
- [[stethoscope diaphragm]] has membrane, [[stethoscope bell]] not. yg bagus littman gt2 bisa depan belakang adult anak, light touch bell, diteken diaphragm
- [[stethoscope bell]] = skin is not tensed (whereas [[stethoscope diaphragm]] flattens out/'tighten up' the skin surface). now, low pitch sounds are lower frequency sounds so they vibrate slower and hence to keep it at their frequency and detect it as such, we use the bell; for high pitch sounds = higher frequency = vibrate faster, snappier, to detect it at that frequency we use diaphragm which makes the skin tight and allow transmitting of the tightness-requiring high frequency. so in other words, if low pitch sounds (e.g. [[murmur]] due to its flappy, loose valve nature rather than tense and snappy normal valve) hits tense surface e.g. skin flattened with diaphragm, what would happen... ->
	- **when low pitch sound meets tight surface, the low frequency wave is not strong enough to vibrate the tight surface, so won't transmit the sound adequately** (or at least will be muffled, dulled...)
		- low pitch sounds? -> [[murmur]] (loose, flappy valves), [[s3 gallop]] [[s4 gallop]],
	- **whereas if high pitch frequency hits loose surface (e.g. skin with bell), the surface won't be tight enough to vibrate as quick as the high frequency wave that hits it, also resulting in sound not transmitted adequately...**
		- high pitch sounds? -> [[lung]] sounds, normal [[heart valve auscultation]] (strong valve snaps)
