# etiopathogenesis
- [[dermatophyte]]s secrete:
	- [[keratinase]] which breaks down [[keratin]] to be eaten as nourishment by the [[dermatophyte]]s
	- [[lipase]] that allows invasion of skin, hair, nails
	- then immune response are triggered -> inflammation etc.
- different skin structures react differently:
	- hairs: hair breakage, [[alopecia]]
	- nails: nails are super keratinized -> crumble, become brittle; meanwhile immunity responses by producing more keratin meant to replace broken ones, but given the messiness of the site, it only makes the nail thicken disorderedly.
	- feet/hands: thick skin -> fissures, scaling
	- groin: thin, moist skin -> ripe for growth and hence more inflammation, itchiness; redness not as neatly annular bcs lots of friction mix things up etc.
	- trunk etc.: normal skin -> annular ring whereby the red rings are inflamed parts and dermatophytes expand but the inner sides are already taken care of by immune response hence clearing.

# tx
Harrison
- topical
	- indication:
		- uncomplicated [[B35.4 tinea corporis]], [[B35.6 tinea cruris or inguinalis]], [[B35.3 tinea pedis (athletes foot)]]
		- not as effective as monotherapy for [[B35.0 tinea barbae and tinea capitis]] or [[B35.1 tinea unguium or onychomycosis]]
		- [[nystatin]] not active against dermatophytes
	- applied 2 dd applic 1 until resolve and continue 1 week beyond resolution
		- [[B35.3 tinea pedis (athletes foot)]] often requires longer; frequently relapses
	- examples
		- [[ketoconazole]]
		- [[clotrimazole]]
		- [[miconazole]]
- systemic
	- [[oral antifungal]]
		- indications
			- those not enough with topical monotherapy: recalcitrant [[B35.3 tinea pedis (athletes foot)]] or [[B35.4 tinea corporis]]; [[B35.0 tinea barbae and tinea capitis]], [[B35.1 tinea unguium or onychomycosis]]
		- precautions
			- fungal must be confirmed first via [[KOH preparation test for fungal infection]] or culture before administration
			- all oral agents may cause [[hepatotoxicity]]
		- contraindications:
			- [[pregnant]] or [[breastfeeding]] women
		- examples ^2681a4
			- [[griseofulvin]]
			- [[itraconazole]]: [[B35.1 tinea unguium or onychomycosis]]
			- [[terbinafine]]
	- [[topical glucocorticoid]]s or [[systemic glucocorticoid]]s for markedly inflam e.g. [[B35.0 tinea barbae and tinea capitis]] to prevent scarring and hair loss
