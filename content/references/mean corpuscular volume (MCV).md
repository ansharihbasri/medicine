
# definition
[[mean corpuscular volume]] definition::laboratory value that measures the average size and volume of a red blood cell ([[RBC]]) ([StatPearls](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK545275/))
<!--SR:!2022-01-13,45,270-->
$$MCV = \frac{Hct \times 10}{[RBC]}$$
$MCV$ = [[mean corpuscular volume]]
$Hct$ = [[hematocrit]]

resources: ([Vernon Louw MedEd](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nxyX_cVbC-E))

# measurements
- via [[blood centrifugation]] -> acquire [[packed cell volume]] (i.e. on the bottom of the flask) -> divide by the number of [[RBC]] ([Vernon Louw MedEd](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nxyX_cVbC-E))
	- [ ] how do you determine [[RBC]] count? (possible relevance for future reading: [[hematocrit#^5f24cf]])
- by directly measuring the size of [[RBC]] via [[peripheral blood smear]] ([Vernon Louw MedEd](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nxyX_cVbC-E)):
	- ![[Pasted image 20211101132756.png]] the diameter of the nucleus of [[lymphocyte]] (in purple) should be roughly the same as the normal diameter of [[RBC]] (in pink/red) around it

# trivia
- a given [[MCV]] can either represent uniformly sized cells or a widely varied sized cells (but with that given mean volume). Such degree of size variation can be shown via [[RDW]]

# causes
- [[microcytic anemia]]
