# concept
![[neutrophil#^cff678]]

"The [[leukocytosis]] occurs initially because of accelerated release of cells from the [[bone marrow]] postmitotic reserve pool (caused by [[cytokine]]s, including [[TNF]] and [[IL-1]]) and is therefore associated with a rise in the number of more immature [[neutrophil]]s in the [[blood]], referred to as a [[shift to the left]]." ([[@kumarRobbinsBasicPathology2018]], p. 87)

"An increase in the number of [[blood]] [[neutrophil]]s is called [[neutrophilia]], and the **presence of immature cells** is termed a [[shift to the left]]. A decrease in the number of [[blood]] [[neutrophil]]s is called [[neutropenia]]." ([[@jamesonHarrisonPrinciplesInternal2018]], p. 394)

"'Shift to the Left' means that there is a shift in the [[WBC]] towards more immature cells (more bands and blasts). This is observed in most bacterial infections and in some [[cancer]]s." ([courses.washington.edu](http://courses.washington.edu/medch535/NEW/PDFs/5.Hematology_WBC.pdf))

"'Shift to the Right' means that there has been a shift back to the normal Diff" ([courses.washington.edu](http://courses.washington.edu/medch535/NEW/PDFs/5.Hematology_WBC.pdf)) ^128e1c

# correlation
- [[infection]] > compensatory [[WBC]] production > [[left-shift]]
- [[bone marrow]] [[tumor]] pushes out immature [[WBC]] out to circulation > [[left-shift]] ([Timothy Sorensen](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gi_soIsGtNo))

# definition
"The standard definition of a left shift is an absolute band form count greater than 7700/microL.[[3]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_shift_(medicine)#cite_note-3) There are competing explanations for the origin of the phrase "left shift," including the left-most button arrangement of early cell sorting machines[[4]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_shift_(medicine)#cite_note-4)[[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_shift_(medicine)#cite_note-5) and a 1920s publication by Josef Arneth, containing a graph in which immature neutrophils, with fewer segments, shifted the median left.[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_shift_(medicine)#cite_note-6) In the latter view, the name reflects a curve's preponderance shifting to the [left](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Body_relative_direction "Body relative direction") on a graph of [hematopoietic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haematopoiesis "Haematopoiesis") cellular [differentiations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_differentiation "Cellular differentiation")." ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Left_shift_(medicine)))
