[[SNPPDI 3A]]

- distribution: regioner
- spread/predilection: hand (palms), foot (soles), mouth (especially oral mucosa). usually oral first then hand/foot
	- mouth may be similar to [[B00 herpes simplex#^201c4e]] (lips)
- types: [[macule]]/[[papule]], (erythematous), [[vesicle]], shallow [[ulcer]] (oral mucosa)
	- similar to:
		- maculopapular, vesicle, crust of:
			- [[B01 varicella (cacar air)#^2fa1c9]] (differ in distribution ([[B01 varicella (cacar air)#^c507b8|generalisata]]), predilection/spread ([[B01 varicella (cacar air)#^13a2fc|sentral then centrifugal]] thus including trunk))
			- [[B02 herpes zoster (cacar air)#^196305]] (may be similarly [[B02 herpes zoster (cacar air)#^06df92|regioner]] but differ in [[B02 herpes zoster (cacar air)#^8c0100|unilateral/dermatomal]])
			- [[L01.01 non-bullous impetigo (impetigo krustosa)]] (may slightly differ in predilection in that HFMD also mouth insides whereas impetigo perioral; also honey-colored crust)
		- papule, vesicle, crust of [[B00 herpes simplex#^0ad4c7]] (may differ in distribution (lokalisata) predilection (esp. genitalia, though may be comparable if lips))
		- maculopapule of:
			- [[B05 measles (rubeola or morbili or campak)]] (differ in distribution (generalisata, and by the time appear in hand/foot, likely trunk, head etc already as well))
			- [[B06 rubella]] (differ in distribution (generalisata, and by the time appear in hand/foot, likely trunk, head etc already as well))

# etiopathogenesis
- [[Coxsackievirus A16]] (most common) or [[Enterovirus 71]] (chatgpt)
- transmission: fecal-oral, respiratory droplets, direct contact with fluid from vesicles
- pathogenesis: viral entry -> replicate in mucosal and lymphoid tissues -> spreads via bloodstream -> targets skin and mucous membrane, cause local inflam there -> vesicle formation
